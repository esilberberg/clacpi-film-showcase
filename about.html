    • The Center for Latin American and Caribbean Studies is pleased to host a program presented by the Coordinadora Latinoaméricana de cine y comunicación de los pueblos indígenas (CLACPI). CLACPI was founded in 1985 and is currently the largest collective of indigenous filmmakers in Latin America. They promote the use of media as a tool of cultural affirmation and social transformation for indigenous communities. CLACPI produces and disseminates audiovisual materials about indigenous cultures and other issues of interest for their collective organizations. They also focus on grassroots media training and workshops to promote agency and self-representation among indigenous communities.

    Highlights of the program include:

    An indigenous film showcase curated by Nelly Kuiru Castro. (Coordinadora, CLACPI Murui from the Maconian Amazon in Colombia); Mariano Estrada (Tseltal, Mexico, President of CLACPI-Mexico); David Hernández Palmar (Wayuu/lipuana Clan, Venezuela, Political Advisor CLACPI)

    El buen vivir, pensar y actuar bien Directed by Nat Iguarán Ipuana, Juan Carlos Jamioy Juagibioy, Nelly Kuiru Castro. Colombia, 2020. A documentary series produced by the National Commission for Communication of Indigenous Peoples (CONCIP)

    Rimana Wasi: Home of Stories Directed by Ximena Málaga Sabogal and Potr Turlej. Prod. Watay Misita, Peru, 2022. Rimana Wasi follows Chaska in her quest to pursue her passion for Quechua storytelling on the radio while also caring for her family
